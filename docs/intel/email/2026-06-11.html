More conviction

The system this week: Bitcoin sits near $63,600 and MicroStrategy near $120. The medium-term trend that governs our hedge is firmly negative, so the regime is bearish and our defensive positions are open — we are past watching for a top and into a live hedge. At the same time, the on-chain stress gauge is falling toward its capitulation extreme, sitting just above the deep-value buy zone but not yet in it. There is no confirmed signal to buy the dip, but it is the thing to watch. So the posture is defensive with a bottom-watch building. The question the voices need to answer: is this weakness the start of a buyable bottom, or just more downside?

What the trusted voices actually said:

James Check is our richest on-chain read, and we lean on his defensive warnings while fading his bottom calls. His note "Conviction Test" describes a grueling bear market where, in his words, "nobody…absolutely nobody cares about Bitcoin," with AI tech stocks sucking the capital out of the room. That is exactly the kind of expert fear that tends to cluster at lows. He is weak at calling bottoms, so this is not a buy trigger, but the tone fits a market grinding into a floor.

Michael Howell reads the liquidity tide — good on direction, weaker on timing. Global liquidity ticked up to $193.5 trillion, but he calls conditions "fragile": the PBoC is tightening faster, Fed liquidity growth is "beginning to fade," and a strong dollar is a drag. He places us in the "speculative" late stage of the liquidity cycle and notes that Bitcoin "recently climbed above $80,000" but has "lately lost momentum." This continues a cautious view rather than marking a new turn, and it is directional, not timed. Net: it supports the defensive posture and gives no reason yet to buy.

The Bitcoin Layer is our one genuine bottom-caller, but its edge only holds while the broad trend hasn't broken — and we ignore their top warnings. Across two posts the author is leaning in. He says he is "loving" the 25% decline, reminds readers their liquidity indicator "flagged a confirmed SELL on May 14th when the bitcoin price was at $80,000," and then asks the title question directly: "Is $60,000 Bitcoin's Bottom?" He describes Friday's timeline as "absolute carnage," full of "fearmongering" and "engagement farming," and casts himself as the calm contrarian. This is the voice we most want for a bottom call. The catch: their bottom edge only holds while the broader trend is still up, and our trend has decisively broken. So read this as a real, on-brand bottom lean from the right specialist, discounted because the regime is against them. And note he has NOT issued a fresh buy signal — the title is a question, not a call. The $80,000 sell he points back to is still in force.

Macro Ops is a good fire alarm and a poor all-clear, so we fade their dip-buying and trust their defensive reads. They are still short BTC, ETH, and SOL, and those shorts "saved our book last week"; they took partial profits Friday. They flag BTC and ETH as sitting at "significant support and deeply oversold," with sentiment "near its lowest reading in two years," but they hold "low conviction in either direction" near term even as a fresh BofA sell signal fires. Their growing conviction is that "we're late cycle" and "setting up for a larger Summer selloff." This is their strong side — a real defensive read off named indicators — and it confirms the hedge. The oversold note is an observation, not a buy call.

Watch two things. First, whether The Bitcoin Layer turns its $60,000 question into a real buy call. Second, the $60,000 area itself — Howell named it as Bitcoin's prior low, and it is the line the on-chain stress gauge is pressing toward.
