Week of June 22, 2026

Same conviction

The system this week: Bitcoin sits near $59,200 and MicroStrategy near $87. The medium-term trend that drives our hedge is firmly down, so the regime is bearish and defensive positions are allowed — we're past watching for a top and into an open hedge. At the same time, our on-chain mean-reversion gauge has fallen into its deep-value extreme, the kind of reading that has marked major bottoms. So the system is pulled two ways: a contrarian buy is arming against a still-falling trend. It's an armed setup, not a confirmed trigger — price has to hold down here, not just touch it. MicroStrategy also trades at a steep discount to the Bitcoin it holds (around 0.67 times), the level below which the company tends to flip toward buying back its own stock rather than issuing it for more Bitcoin. The one question for the voices this week: is this a buyable bottom forming, or is more downside coming?

What the trusted voices actually said:

Michael Howell — Two posts, both on currencies and central-bank policy, neither a Bitcoin call. His read on the new Fed chair is that Warsh won't hike fast but will happily let monetary conditions tighten on their own — "they will let the market do the heavy lifting" — which he says points to higher bond yields and a firmer dollar. A stronger dollar and rising yields are a headwind for risk assets like Bitcoin, not a tailwind. There's no buy-the-dip here and no easing signal; if anything, the backdrop he's describing blows the wrong way for a bottom. He's better on direction than timing, so weight this modestly. Net: leans against the buy, mildly supports the defensive posture.

The Bitcoin Layer — This is the voice we'd most want to hear from on a possible bottom, and they did not call one. They frame the present as a bear market — "a long siege" — and explicitly tell readers to wait: "the bear-market framework and the new Rookie/Veteran data still argue for patience." The one constructive note is that the price levels Bitcoin needs to reclaim are dropping toward $70,000, which they say "is good." But that's the battlefield improving, not a turn. Their read on bottoms carries the most weight when the broader trend is still intact, and they themselves are calling this a confirmed bear — so even our best buy-side source is sitting on its hands. No bottom call, no pivot. That absence is the loudest thing in the window.

Macro Ops — Their long post is mostly a bullish macro thesis: accelerating growth, sticky inflation, and a market that "looks to me like it wants to trade up." That optimism is their weaker suit, so we discount it. The part that carries weight is the fresh caution off named indicators: "the sentiment and positioning backdrop is pretty bearish and we're setting up for what will likely be a significant corrective period," with the BofA Sell Signal flashing and small traders loading up on puts at "one of the highest proportions of hedging in 25yrs." That defensive turn is the trustworthy half of their note, and it supports the hedge.

James Check — No posts in the window.

Lyn Alden — No posts in the window.

Willy Woo — No posts in the window.

Does the narrative confirm the system? On the defensive side, yes — quietly. Howell's stronger-dollar, higher-yields read and Macro Ops' fresh warning of a significant corrective period both lean the way the hedge does. On the buy side, the picture is more interesting. The voice we'd most trust to call this bottom — The Bitcoin Layer — explicitly refused to, telling readers to stay patient in a confirmed bear. But that refusal isn't a strike against the buy. Their read on bottoms is most reliable when the trend is still intact, so it doesn't carry weight in a broken one anyway. And at a deep-value extreme like this, widespread caution and silence is exactly what bottoms look like — the crowd can't buy the low. Three of six voices going dark, plus the bottom-caller standing down, is the texture of capitulation, not a refutation of it. So nobody confirms the buy, but nobody credibly argues it away either.

Bottom line: SAME conviction. The trend-and-hedge side got mild corroboration; the buy side got the expected fearful silence rather than a green light — which neither raises nor lowers a contrarian setup that was always going to arm before any human confirmed it. The one thing that would genuinely add buy-side conviction — The Bitcoin Layer calling a bottom — did not happen, so the buy stays an armed setup, not a trigger. Watch two things: whether Bitcoin holds this deep-value zone (a clean break of recent lows kills the setup), and The Bitcoin Layer's $70,000 reclaim level — if that starts getting taken back, the bear framework they're leaning on begins to crack.
