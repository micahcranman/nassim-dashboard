Week of June 15, 2026

Same conviction

The system this week: Bitcoin is near $62,900 and MicroStrategy near $113. The trend that governs our hedge is firmly down, so we're defensive and the hedge is open — past watching for a top, into an active bearish stance. At the same time, the on-chain stress gauge is sliding toward its capitulation extreme, sitting just above the deep-value buy zone. There's no confirmed signal to buy weakness yet, but it's the thing to watch. MicroStrategy itself trades at a steep discount to the Bitcoin it holds (about 0.82 times), low enough that the company's own playbook would tilt toward buying back stock rather than issuing it. So we're defensive with a bottom-watch building. The question the voices need to answer: is this falling market close enough to a real bottom to start trusting the buy side?

What the trusted voices actually said:

James Check is our richest on-chain read — strong when he warns, less reliable when he calls a low. His "Momentum Sprouts" note concedes the tape is ugly: "the monthly chart is red, the weekly chart is red, and the daily chart is red." But he argues the rate of change matters more than the level, and "to my eye it is starting to tell a different story." He frames the moment as late-stage capitulation grinding out weak hands, leaving "a hardened holder-base of tenured HODLers." Read against where the on-chain gauge actually sits — pinned near its capitulation extreme — this is not a brave bottom call to fade. It's the on-chain specialist describing the exact capitulation dynamics our gauge is flagging. That makes it quiet confirmation of the bottom-watch, not a conflict, and not yet a buy signal.

Lyn Alden — quiet. No posts in the window.

Michael Howell reads the liquidity tide — good on direction, weak on timing. His weekly update is mixed-to-cautious. Global liquidity edged up to $193.7 trillion, but he flags softening risk appetite and says we're "in the 'speculative' late-stage of the liquidity cycle where risk assets become more vulnerable to setbacks," calling for "a more selective investment approach." He notes Bitcoin "recently climbed above US$80,000 supported by the Q1 liquidity expansion, but have lately lost momentum." This is direction, not timing, and the lean is defensive — consistent with our open hedge, not a bottom call.

Macro Ops is a good fire alarm and a poor all-clear, so we lean on their warnings and fade their dip-buying. Their note is two-sided, but the durable signal is bearish: intermediate and longer-term indicators are "flashing elevated fragility and a high probability of a cyclical top within the next 12 months." The fresh, named catalyst is net equity supply flipping positive for the first time since 2021 — buybacks no longer outrunning issuance, so "a structural tailwind is now a headwind." Their short-term bullishness rests on a ceasefire juicing risk assets, which is exactly the dip-buy lean we fade. The newly-flagged structural top warning is the part that carries weight, and it backs the defensive side.

The Bitcoin Layer is our one genuine bottom-caller, but their edge only holds while the broader trend is still up — and ours has broken. Three posts, constructive in tone. They expect "a reduction in volatility from the Spring months" and a summer that should "dampen some outsized, downside risks." They push back on the idea that Strategy must unwind before Bitcoin can recover. And most concretely, they published a post titled "Unconfirmed Green Dot" — an early, not-yet-confirmed turn on their liquidity signal. That's their good side: a lean toward a buyable low. The catch is the one that matters here. Their bottom edge depends on the broader trend still pointing up, and ours has decisively broken, so we discount it. Note the operative word: unconfirmed.

Willy Woo — quiet. No posts in the window.

Does the narrative confirm the system? The defensive side gets clean corroboration. Howell and Macro Ops — the two voices we trust on warnings — both lean cautious, and Macro Ops did it off a freshly-named indicator, which is when they're worth most. The buy side is softer but not empty. Check is describing capitulation playing out, and The Bitcoin Layer flagged an unconfirmed early turn — but Check's read is an observation of the process rather than a firm call, and TBL's bottom edge evaporates in a confirmed downtrend like this one. So no voice gives us a buy signal we'd act on yet. And here's the subtle part: with the on-chain gauge pinned near capitulation, the fact that even our bottom-callers are tentative is itself consistent with the setup. The crowd can't bring itself to buy the low — that's what a low tends to feel like.

Bottom line: SAME conviction. The defensive posture is well-supported, and the bottom-watch is alive but not yet confirmed by anyone we'd trust to call it. Watch two things: whether the on-chain stress gauge actually touches its capitulation extreme — the trigger we're waiting on — and whether The Bitcoin Layer's unconfirmed turn becomes a confirmed one, since that's the buy-side voice whose pivot would genuinely matter even after we discount it for the broken trend. A close below the recent Bitcoin lows would deepen the hedge. A confirmed signal from The Bitcoin Layer, plus the gauge hitting its extreme, is what would flip us toward the buy.
